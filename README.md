## What Is This?

Massachusetts towns are deploying Flock Safety license plate reader cameras that connect to a **national surveillance network of 5,000+ law enforcement agencies**. These reports analyze public records requests to understand how these systems are actually being used — who's searching, what justifications are given, and whether local policies are being followed.

## Key Findings Across Towns

- **Small-town cameras feed a national network.** A 3-camera deployment in Stow, MA connects to 5,000+ agency networks nationwide. Every vehicle photographed is queryable by officers from Dallas to Sarasota.
- **ICE and immigration enforcement flows through the network.** Despite local policies excluding immigration enforcement, the shared network carries hundreds of deportation warrant, ICE warrant, and immigration violation searches from agencies in other states.
- **Search justifications are often inadequate.** Searches with reasons like "susp", "bolo" (no specifics), street names, and "Solicitors" lack the "probable cause" required by local policy.
- **Flock actively markets protest surveillance** to police chiefs.
