## What Is This Document?

This is the Littleton Police Department's response to a public records request about their Flock Safety ALPR system. Unlike Stow's 23,922-page response, Littleton's response is a set of **14 individual documents** — contracts, invoices, emails, procurement forms, and Flock corporate paperwork. Notably absent: **any search audit logs, ALPR policies, or data sharing agreements.**

---

## Key Finding: This System Is Brand New

Littleton's Flock contract was **signed June 26, 2025**. The first invoice was issued **February 14, 2026**. The cameras may not even be fully operational yet. This means Littleton is in the rare position where **citizens can still shape the rules before the system is entrenched.**

## How It Was Funded

The $15,000 first year was **not paid from the police budget**. It was funded by a **Public Safety Grant from the Littleton Electric Light and Water Department (LELWD)**.

From Chief Pinard's email to LELWD General Manager Nick Lawler (June 2, 2025):

> *"The Littleton Police Department is currently evaluating the Flock Safety camera system — an advanced platform designed to capture license plates and vehicle data in real-time... We would like to explore the possibility of using the public safety grant to fund the first year of this initiative. It is my intention to support the ongoing costs through our department's operating budget in subsequent years."*

The Police Detail Turnover document (July 17, 2025) confirms: **$15,000 from "Safety Grant - Flock Devices"** was received via check #354365 from LELWD.

> [!WARNING]
> The LELWD Board approved this grant at their **June 25, 2025 meeting** — one day before the contract was signed. This is a utility board, not the Select Board or Town Meeting. **$15,000 in surveillance technology was funded through a utility grant with no apparent public hearing or town-wide vote.**

## Sole Source Justification

Flock was procured as a **sole source** — meaning no competitive bidding. Two documents support this:

### Flock's Own Letter (June 23, 2025)
Flock CEO Garrett Langley's letter claims Flock is *"the sole provider of our proprietary public safety technology"* and that *"No other vendor offers the specific combination of hardware and cloud-based features."*

The letter also reveals the regional network pitch:
> *"Flock Safety provides the ability to access additional cameras from Flock customers, including: **Groton, Stow, Chelmsford, Billerica, Lowell, Wilmington**, at no additional cost."*

### Town Procurement Form (October 23, 2025)
The Town's own procurement form lists the justification as "Sole Source developer and provider of this proprietary technology." The three vendor quote fields are left blank — **no alternatives were evaluated.**

> [!NOTE]
> The sole source claim is debatable. Competitors like Motorola/Vigilant, Rekor, Genetec, and others offer ALPR systems. The "sole source" framing is Flock Safety's own marketing language, adopted verbatim by the department.

## What's Missing — And Why It Matters

### No ALPR Policy

Stow PD at least has Policy #9.07 (even if they don't fully follow it). Littleton appears to have **no written policy governing**:
- Who can access the system
- What justifications are required for searches  
- When data must be deleted
- How audits are conducted
- Whether immigration enforcement is excluded
- What happens when the system generates false positives

### No Data Sharing Agreements

The contract grants Littleton access to the **nationwide Flock network** with "10 billion additional plate reads per month." But there's no documented agreement about:
- Which agencies can access Littleton's camera data
- What purposes other agencies can use it for
- Whether ICE, CBP, or fusion centers have access
- Whether any agencies have been denied access

### No Audit Logs

The system may be too new to have substantial logs, but the absence of even a framework for auditing is concerning.

## Key Questions for Citizens

1. **Does Littleton PD have an ALPR policy?** If not, who decided it was acceptable to deploy surveillance technology without governance rules? If so, why wasn't it provided in response to this records request?

2. **Who approved this?** A utility board funded year one. Did the Select Board or Town Meeting ever vote on deploying license plate readers? Will the $15,000/year recurring cost come before Town Meeting for approval?

3. **Why sole source?** Flock's own CEO wrote the sole source letter. The town's procurement form cites Flock's marketing verbatim. Were alternatives genuinely evaluated?

4. **What are the data sharing rules?** Littleton's cameras will feed into the same national network where [Stow's analysis found 723 ICE/immigration enforcement searches](../stow/). What protections exist?

5. **Where are the cameras going?** The invoice references a Flock planning portal link. Have camera locations been publicly disclosed?

6. **What search justification standards apply?** Without a policy, any officer with a login can search the nationwide network for any reason, with no case number requirement and no review process.

7. **Is there a sunset clause?** The contract auto-renews for 24 months unless cancelled 30 days before expiration. Once installed, removing these cameras requires affirmative action.
